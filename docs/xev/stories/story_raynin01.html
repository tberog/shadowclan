Teachings from a priest - By Raynin!

As a cleric of Innoruuk, many people, dark or light, have many expectations of me. Many seek me out, in hopes of a quick heal, or perhaps a blessing from Innoruuk. What they fail to remember, however, is that I am evil, full of hatred and that I cater to none of them...Unless it benefits myself, of course. So there I was, a young cleric (contrary to terms), practicing my skills on various dark elves I passed. Once my skills were learned to a certain degree, I kept them to myself until needed. Unfortunately I seemed to have contracted a few followers who knew of my skills along the way. I'm thinking that I need to rid myself of these followers, as they are of no benefit to my journey. There I stood, on the bridge of Nektulos Forest, having a rather intense conversation with one of the guards about the 2 humans he had recently slain who were trying to cross his bridge. His story was rather amusing, and therefore had my full attention as a young lad approached me, nearly falling at my feet. I take note of this, and wonder why people are always crawling on the ground around me. Are they wanting healed perhaps? Hmmm. I step over him and continue talking with the guard. "poisoned" was all the lad could say. So I look down upon him, hold out my hand and ask, "got a gold piece?" He promised a gold piece when he was able to move again, so I cured his poisoning and received my payment then continued with my conversation with the guard. "could you please heal me?" he asks. "Got a gold piece?" was my reply. And so he hands me another gold piece. "Sit...and you will be healed, child" Needless to say, he was not pleased with this "cleric" and so told me so. He told me of how I'm the meanest person he has met yet. I bowed and thanked him. Just as soon as I thought I was all alone again in my travels, this young lad approaches me again, this time much more experienced than the last time I saw him. I gave him the blessings of Innoruuk this time, and a gift to go with it. No longer is he an enemy, and also no longer is he a follower. He is now my equal, and has learned well.
