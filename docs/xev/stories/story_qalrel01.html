The Ghuvor

A long time ago in Cabilis, a young eggling was hatched. She was named Qalrel, by her mother, Nallena Zhatal. As a child, she was told stories of her father, though she never really knew him. Such things were said as that he was a great warrior, others stated that he was a cowardly thief, and some stated he was long dead. Qalrel did not know what to make of these tales, and her mother would not utter a word about Qalrel's father... until one day.
"Mother, Cazic-Thule may ssmile today, for our enemies will now only know fear! I hass been accepted under the tutilage of the warriorss!"

Her mother just looked at her for a long time, until at last, a smile came, accompanied by a tear.

"Child, tssu are tssur father's daughter."

Then and there, time seemed to stand still.

"Tssu sspoke of father... perhapss thiss meanss tssu will tell more?"

Nallena Zhatal, a woman of pride, was now teary eyed and trembling. A few moments later, her hands steadied, and she spoke.

"Tssur father... hiss name wass Oerngl. He wass a proud warrior. He wanted tssu to have thiss."

She stood up and took out a small pouch. This was an article that Qalrel had seen her mother with often, and always she clasped it tightly, and held it to her heart. She opened the small pouch, and took out a dull dark grey thing.
"Thiss wass what he left uss when he wass ssummoned to war."

She flipped it over and upon a smoothened surface, there was an insignia in a crystalline material.

"He never told me what it wass, or what it wass for, or even what it ssayss. But nonetheless, when tssu took tssur place with the warriorss, he wanted tssu to have it."

Nallena handed the small artifact to her daughter. Qalrel looked deep into it, every facet, every little indentation, and especially the detail. She wanted to destroy it, for she felt a burning rage of mistrust, but at the same time wished to cherish it, for it was as her father had wished. She held it in her hand, then enveloped it in her claws, and clenched her fists.

"Why wass I not told earlier? Why did tssu wait until now?"

Nallena frowned, and with sad eyes, looked upon her daughter.

"Freedom of choice, what if we told tssu tssu would be a warrior, wouldn't tssu want ssome ssay in the matter? It wass what tssur father wanted, and alsso, becausse now it can be ssecure, that tssu are in alliance with the Legionnairess of Cabiliss."

Qalrel looked down at the insignia, and then at her mother, then back to the insignia. She clasped it tightly, and went to the Legionnaire trainers for her first assignment, without another word to her mother since that night. Still, she wonders if she made the right choice, leaving without a single word.
