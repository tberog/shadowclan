General

Make a few macros of monster speech. Incorporate words from our Language Lists. Never use humie profanity. Stay in character. Too many Out Of Character (OOC) comments can ruin the feel of the game. Get drunk. Duel with your fellow monsters to prove who is the biggest and meanest. Humans and other Good races are to be harassed on sight. But make it all in fun, do not ruin theirs to create fun for yourself. We have worked very hard to let people know we are serious roleplayers and we have a tradition of quality roleplaying to uphold. Follow the rules listed here and listen to clan members ranked higher than you. Disobey Shadowclan leaders at your own peril!. Disobeying them repeatedly can result in actual banishment from the Shadowclan. Most importantly, have fun and don't ruin others' fun.

Honor Code

  • Shadowclan tries not to ruin the fun of others.
    • Shadowclan harasses goody races, and even the evil races if bored.
  • Shadowclan tries to help those in need without leaving character if at all possible. Never turn down a request for assistance from a player, unless they have been labeled as a bad humie or such.
  • Shadowclan always follows the clan chain of command.

Things Shadowclan does NOT do

  • Shadowclan will never use external macro programs.
  • Shadowclan does not abuse any known bugs and will report any bugs found. This is to preserve the game balance for all.
  • Shadowclan does not use humie curses, vulgarity, or profanity.
  • Shadowclan does not normally have humie friends.
  • Shadowclan does not concern themselves with faction for good races. All light races and their allies are subject to death at our whim. Those that need to gain some form of faction for quests or something of that nature must do so with a valid rp reason. Not a quick excuse. These cases will be handled on a one on one basis as they arise.
  • Shadowclan does not ninja loot.
  • Shadowclan does not train others.
  • Shadowclan does not kill steal.

Loot Rules

  • SINGLE GROUP: If on an ALL CLAN hunt... any loot acquired will go to a member in the group that will USE IT. If more then one will use, then will random amongst the ones able to use it. Else it stays in clan for auction or passing on to someone who can use it in clan (yes, this rule carries over to multi-group raids as well). If not a clan group then random as usual.
  • Multi-Group raids: On any raid which requires more than one group of 6, and which the Shadowclan dictates the loot policies, the following rules apply:

Need Over Greed:

When an item drops, all those who are present that can use and would use the item will /random for it, being careful NOT to /random until the raid leader has said so, with the victor getting the spoils. Once a person has won an item on that particular raid, they are excluded from future /randoms on that raid until everyone of that class has attained an item. The raid leader or designated loot caller will call out when to roll and for what item.

Guidelines:

  1. Loot is determined by a /random roll, with the highest roll winning the prize.
  2. The numbers rolled for an item are decided on by the Raid Leader or the person he/she designates. Following exceptions to the norm:
    a) Must post attendance on raid posts, or will be reated as late..
    b) If late or didnt respond to raid post then 1 hour wait until can roll.
    c) Raid leader will call out classes to roll on an item if the item is an all/all item.
  3. Only group leaders are to look into corpses or for the loot.
    Everyone that is on the raid is entitled to a fair chance for loot, both Clan members and invites. On the other hand, those that participate on the "break" of a particular raid may be entitled to additional benefits, as determined by the raid leader and stated BEFORE the raid. (this can be +200 to rolls, etc)
  4. If an item drops that your class can use but you do not want, you are not required to random for it. You can also roll and if you win it, offer it to another on the raid that can use it.
  5. You are only eligible to /random for an item if:
    a) You have not already won something on that trip or
    b) All those that could use the item had already won something on that trip.
  6. There may be special situations where an item will be given to a particular person without a /random. Example: an epic quest item drops and it's the last piece that a particular person needs for the quest, this must be stated by the raid leader PRIOR to the beginning of that particular raid.
  7. When in doubt, the raid leader has final say, backed by the attending guild officers. If you have a question, refer back to this rule.

Notes and Exceptions:

  • Late Players: Late players can not roll for an item until they have been a part of the raid for one hour. They need to confirm time they arrived with the raid leader so both on same page as to when that hour is up. As always everyone is encouraged to show up on time to make forming groups, buffing, etc, go as smooth as possible and without delays. Obviously this is not always possible and it is better late then never. Attempts to get late players into raid will be made at the raid’s convenience, but sometimes it will just not be possible for numerous reasons such as no porters, no CoH, raid to deep, etc.
  • Alt Players and Loot: When people do a raid they are encouraged to have one character. If a person has two or more attending a raid, using two or more computers, they decided which one would be their main for looting purposes. Obviously it is poor form to have a second logged in character chosen as an alt rolling against mains, especially when the alt’s main is playing at the same time on the same raid. If something is going to rot then logging in another to loot or deferring to an alt also makes sense. Basically rolling for loot is one player per raid and not per character on a raid when a person is playing more then one character at the same time. When someone volunteers for a CRITICAL raid role to bring alt along for raid instead of a main, then the alt for THAT raid should roll as anyone on raid does since they are there in a critical raid role MAIN roll. A typical example is an alt cleric needing to be played because there are no other clerics. No one is REQUIRED to play any particular character, but volunteering is never discouraged when there is a critical need. Alt characters are also expected to take a back seat to mains when the need arises to have alt for critical classes and a main show up at a later time.
  • Raid Roles for Alts and Invites. During raids there are some specific roles assumed by players and classes. Examples often included puller(s), raid organizer, enchanter(s) (speed/clarity), shaman (slow/buffing), necro (buffing/dots) etc. Invites and alts will defer by defaults they roles to clans and mains, unless an agreement is worked to the clan or mains satisfaction. Just because an invite or alt is a high level then a clan member on the raid does not imply they get the role filled automatically.
  • Participation: Participation is expected to not show favoritism to classes. Example clan clerics are expected to participate in fear raids even though their class armor does not drop there.
  • Invites: If you want to invite someone on a clan raid check with the raid leader and get it approved. If there is a sign up for the raid on the message board have them post. Other evil races, friends of the clan, and small guilds with the same play style of the clan’s are welcome to attend clan functions so long as raid leader is aware and approves.
  • God Items: All non-epic God items will be straight randomed to all that can use it regardless if they had already won an item. (God's only spawn once a week, and their items are not counted as plane gear.) If multiple god item drop winners will not take in future rolls for god items during the remainder of the raid. One god item per player per raid.
  • Epic Quest Items: These quest items will go to the highest leveled race/class that needs it the most. Clan members working on their epics are expected to take an active role in organizing and leading raids for their epic. The clan’s resource are available to help acquire epics but it is not the clan’s responsibility to lead and organize raid just for individual’s to get their epics. On a normal hunt and much like the general rule of, "If it's the last piece you need to complete your planar suit, you get that piece", there will ba a similar stipulation in Epic drops. If someone completed, for example, 90% of their epic and needed a piece or two to finish - AND Another character was only 30% done with theirs, then the one most complete with quest would get it. Note, If someone organizes the raid to get a quest piece, they would get the piece when it drops.
  • Spells: IF a spell drops from a creature. All who are present that can currently cast the spell or are within ONE level of being able to cast the spell will random for the spell. If none qualify then the spell will be banked in the Clan Vault and given freely to the first Clan member to reach the appropriate age to Scribe it.
  • Non-Rare Gems/items: Non-rare gems and items (such as emerald rings, pages, etc.) are looted by group leaders. Group leader then give these gems/items to the group cleric to cover the expenses of their reagents required to buff the tanks. Pages and Words are handed out to the appropriate caster class.
  • Rare Gems: All rare gems, diamond, blue diamond, jancinth, and black sapphires are turned over to a clan representative to be use for jewelry making. Jewelry made from clan raids will either go back to the raid participants or auctioned off to clan member on the message board.
  • Breastplates and Robes: Do NOT count towards the "1 Item Only" rule. They are the exception.
    Example: If "Necrobob" wins a Bracer and then a BP drops later, "Necrobob" can also /random for the BP. If "Necrojohn" wins a BP first however, "Necrojohn" can not roll for the Bracer if it drops afterwards. The Breastplate/Robe counts as the 1 Drop IF it is won first. So it is possible that one Raid Member can win 2 pieces before anyone else wins one IF the BP/Robe was the second item won. But, from prior experience... this is a one in thousand scenario.
  • The Final Piece of a Set: If an item drops that would complete a persons full set of his class's armor, then that person gets that drop by default. The exception to this rule is a Breastplate or Robe. Those are /randomed as usual. Though this may sound unfair at first, remember that the person who needs only one item will not touch the other items of that class that may drop. So others of same class have better chance to obtain those pieces.
  • Plane of Fear: Cryosilk pieces will be randomed to Enchanters and Mages first.
  • Plane of Hate: Cryosilk pieces from chests will be randomed to Necros and Wizards.

Anon and Roleplay Switch

  • Shadowclan prohibits the use of the "/Anon" filter. The use of this hides you from the other members of Shadowclan as well hides your Guild tag from view, Guild Tags are to be displayed with pride at all times.
  • The "/roleplay" switch DOES NOT HIDE YOU FROM /WHO GUILD ALL any longer. Recent changes by Verant now allow us to see any member even while using /roleplay. So, /roleplay is allowed for Peon ranks and above. The Scrubs may not use it since they are being watched by the Captains during this period.
  • The Planes: While a clansman is up in any of the Planes, they are allowed to use the /roleplay filter in order to hide location from the casual viewer. Some people look to see who is in what Planes then they may run in to take advantage of the other / first groups hard work. To prevent this event, /roleplay is allowed regardless of Rank while IN the Plane. Also, Planes are high lag areas so the less people rushing in the better.

The parting of ways

There are those that leave the clan to follow their own desires and paths. Many go, many return. Those that were once in Shadowclan and wish to return will begin anew and will lose a RANK to prove their worth to clan once more. There will be no need for review or any such judgments if the clansmen left of their own free will before and on good terms. But those that were forcibly removed must request to return and the leadership will discuss this and then take a vote on the acceptance or denial of the member.
