Description:

small, smart, snobbish

Dark Elves, dark of skin and dark by nature. They often follow evil purposes, devoting themselves fanatically to secret causes. Though they are weak in body, they are strong in the use of magic. Dark Elves and elves hate one another passionately, for there are ancient enmities between the two races. The two are fey cousins, however, and share an enormously long life span. Barring an accidental or violent death, Dark elves will normally live until they tire of life.

Dark elves plan their attacks down to the last person standing and what spell they should cast at what time. They have the Mind power and they use it effectively. They think of Ogres and Trolls as Pawns in there over all plans. They simply have to influence the right person and they can get almost anything they want done. Dark elves are said to be able to manipulate the gods if they so desired.

Cleric:

Death, Destruction, Hate, and healers of life are an odd combination of the powers that is given to the devoted Priests of Innoruuk. Some put us in the same category as the Necromancer. That can’t be farther from the truth. The Necromancer is a formidable ally also bearing the powers of their deity but they are the masters of death and decay and use their powers to forward that cause. With undead creatures being at your mercy, you can slay them with ease. Priests of Innoruuk help the warriors of our cause with healing and aid, or causing the destruction to those who stand in the way of Innoruuk’s will. Innoruuk (The prince of Hate) has given those of strong belief in him a portion of his mighty powers to use in battle and to heal those fighting for his ultimate cause. We also have strength and a fighting ability to match some of the best warriors around. Our motivations are our dying beliefs in Innoruuk and his powers that some day he will take us to serve him personally on his plane of existence. Our drive as Dark Elf Priests is to become the one true power under our god. To use others in our climb to the top and crush those that stand in our way.

How to role play a Dark Elf Cleric:

The first thing to know, and I say this from experience, is that your Wisdom must be high. Yes, you can crack that cracked staff over something’s head with great strength, but people rely on your skills to keep them alive during combat. So with your intelligence and strength combined, you will do the job that Innoruuk has sent you to do. Some say your disadvantage is your evilness, but think of that as being your advantage. (evil grin)

As a priest of Innoruuk, you’re expected to keep those around you alive, but don’t let this become your sole purpose in life. You also have personal goals to fulfill, and quests to go on too. But while you’re with your brethren, do pay close attention to detail. Learn for yourself what works best, when to heal and when to kill. You’re not a magician, so get in the battle if you can, but keep track of those around you, don’t let them die. I, personally, will take a few good whacks at the enemy, then back out and start healing my warriors if they need it. Once I find that I can’t heal anymore, I yell my warning to those trusting their lives to me, as loudly as I can, and with great meaning. I jump into battle again, hitting the enemy with all my might, letting out great roars with every blow. I find that I can do as much if not more damage than those fighting alongside me, so why be afraid to fight just because I can cast spells? Depending on how the battle is going, I may even find myself sitting in the midst of it all to say a quick prayer to Innoruuk. Praying that He might bless me with just one more healing to save a dying comrade of mine, or that I might have just one more blast at my prey in hopes of slaying the beast. The priest is usually the one who calls for retreat, then may or may not stay behind to ensure the safety of others. Earn the trust and respect of others so that you will be able to make this call effectively. Calling for retreat is a big decision, and shouldn’t be done with haste.

After a long hard battle, you’ll most likely feel the need to find a quiet spot to sit and pray to Innoruuk…to not only thank Him for allowing you success, but to also ask for more power so that you may continue to follow his command successfully.

May your hatred be your guide..

Enchanter:

Why do it yourself when someone else can. These words are what the Dark Elf Enchanter lives by. The enchanter is very powerful in charming other creatures to do his bidding and also in making weapons magically enhanced for their allies. They do carry an array of offensive spells but in this category they still do not match the ability of the wizard. Of the mystics they seem to spend most of their time studying and scribing.

How to role play a Dark Elf Enchanter:

  • Use any means to stay out of the front lines of any fight. If someone tries make them pay!
  • You are kind of a "utility" of sorts, it seems you have almost all the different types of spells available...even some very unique ones.

Magician:

Dark Elf Magicians are the summoners of the arcane arts, and wield the power to call forth everything from a loaf of bread to a huge Fire Elemental to fight for them. Although not as powerful as some of the other mystics when it comes to offensive firepower the Magician has a larger variety of spells.

How to role play a Dark Elf Magician:

  • You have the ability to summon others to fight for you ... so do it!
  • Of the mystic types you probably get along with non-mystic types better than most except for maybe the enchanter.

Rogue:

This is one group of Dark Elves that you do not want to upset. This group has members that are thieves, assassins even some that are more of your warrior types. The have some special abilities that make them one of the most unique group of Dark Elves in Neriak. There are ways around Neriak that only the Rogues understand and they would sooner slit your throat than trust you with their locations. They are a valuable member of your group ... if you can somewhat gain their trust. No matter what a rogue tells you remember one thing....he is in it for one thing and one thing only.... Himself!

How to role play a Dark Elf Rogue:

  • The world revolves around you. There's a reason for everything that happens and usually somewhere in the future a profit is to be made because of it.
  • There's no need to get out in front and be a punching bag while there are others that are willing to do so. Sometimes its better to sneak around behind the opponent and attack.
  • Secrets, Secrets, Secrets aren't they just great!
  • You'll put up with almost anything or anyone if you see that there might be something to gain because of it.

Shadowknight:

Somewhere in the depths of Neriak is a place where the wails and shrieks of the tormented echo through the vaulting caverns of our deep city. Here is a place where all the stone has been stained in blood, and where the suffering of the pathetic and helpless saturates the air, intoxicating. This place is the house of the Shadowknights of Neriak, where they work side by side with Neriak's Necromancers, the others who share our passion for death and for pain. Why we come to this place to learn the arts of violence is different for each Tier'dal who sets foot in the bloodied chambers. Some, to bring suffering upon the light races who seek to stifle our glory. Others, to follow the path of their ancestors, and to serve Innoruuk faithfully, bringing death as their fathers before them once did. And others, like myself, simply because they delight in the torment of others, and seek to destroy every living thing upon the face of Norrath. . .life is indeed a sickening thing.

Shadowknights share the arcane knowledge of our Necromancer brethren to bring a greater efficiency to their dispensation of death. We study side by side with them, but preferring the feeling of blood upon our flesh, we seek first to learn how to bring our victims to their knees through perfection of the use of implements of pain. We are not content to kill with spells from afar, we prefer the frenzy and carnage of the battlefield. We wish to feel the squelch of spilled entrails beneath our feet, we wish to look into the eyes of our victims as we send them to oblivion, to savor the fear and rage that lie within at the moment of death. We use our arcane knowledge to enrage our victims, and to steal their life forces, so that we may better subdue them by the sword, or to call up slaves from beyond the graves to fight by our side, and die at our bidding.

Regardless of the reason why we have chosen our path, all Shadowknights share a common bond, of violence, and devotion to the Shadow Gods, to further Innoruuk's glorious plans for Norrath. We all share in our hate of the pale filth that lives upon the surface, content in their false gods, and false peace. As Shadowknights, we all seek the destruction of this pestilence, to cleanse it from the face of Norrath, even if we are ourselves destroyed in the process. We render all unto Innoruuk, our swords and our lives, and do his bidding even if it means certain death. More often, the rivvil do find that Innoruuk's bidding means their death, and the death of their children, and not our own. The day shall come when they cower before us, faces in the bloodied dirt begging for mercy, and mercy they shall not have, only oblivion.

How to role play a Dark Elf Shadowknight:

  • Although each Shadowknight has their own reason for following their chosen path, they all share a common hatred for the light races of Norrath, and would do most anything to harm them, especially the followers of the false Gods of Light.
  • Shadowknights frequently choose their path due to a fanatic devotion to the teachings of the Shadow Gods, and are thus vicious and cruel by nature, and delight in the suffering of others. They need no reason to kill, rather, they are usually compelled to do so.
  • Shadowknights are primarily a melee class, their arcane powers being used for a tool to help support their melee abilities, not as their main offensive. They can be very helpful if wisely administered, but the Shadowknight must always remember that their primary strength is in their swords and in their hate.
  • Shadowknights have the ability to call upon undead slaves to aid them in melee, but they must remember to use these slaves wisely. They will always be much weaker than the Shadowknight, and if sent in alone, will nearly always be killed. However, they do provide quite the perfect sacrificial lamb...
  • Shadowknights are granted a power that brings grievous pain and the most intense suffering to their enemies, but they may only use this harm touch once per day. They must learn to use it wisely, and only in cases where death is imminent. It is foolish to rely upon it for offensive power, for it will frequently be resisted by creatures with a much greater power than the Shadowknight.

Wizard:

Dark Elf Wizards seek knowledge and they are so focused in accomplishing this that no one better get in their way. They are the masters of the offensive spell and they use this ability to cause destruction to all those that are not of a dark race. When it comes to the ability to cause magical damage you need look no further than the pits of Neriak for this Evil being.

How to role play a Dark Elf Wizard:

  • Two words "Offensive Firepower"
  • You're looking in the wrong place if you're looking for someone to put a defensive spell on you. You might end up with a Fire Bolt up your butt!
  • Socializing may not be your strong point but almost anyone will put up with you because when it comes to a battle and dishing out damage it is your specialty.
