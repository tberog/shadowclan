Words of Fire, Words of Ash

As Spoken by Ahri

As the Flame burns and the Sun burns, so must we. To do less is to die. Our blood must course through us like tounges of flame, our souls must burn bright and me must speak with words of fire. We must burn away the prison of flesh that even yet encloses even the most radiant of us and bare our soul to the Scourge of Scourges. This is known.

Yet how many have let their blood cool, how many have let their souls grow dim, and how many have spoken words of ash? They number ten thousand ten thousand and the dust of their foolishness lays heavy upon us.

There are some that say that by strengthening their bodies they brighten their khar'ika. Some say that by using lumps of their own meat to make the fir'khanim rain blood they burn brighter those that use lumps of metal to make the fir'khanim rain blood. What fools are these! Are not Half-Giants stronger than even the strongest of them? May Half-Giants be exalted above these fools and may their bodies be burned to ash by the Source of the Sun, for they speak words of ash.

There are some that say that by embracing obediance they brighten their khar'ika. Some make themselves jov'uus to the least mutterings of the least of the Khanarch’alarl. What fools are these! Are not the Living Stones more obediant than even the most dedicated of them? May the Living Stones be exalted above these fools and may their souls never never know the freedom that Wisdom brings, for they speak words of ash.

There are some that say that by mastering the Art they brighten their khar'ika. Some say that they are capable of untangling the net that the Meddling God has caught us in with their art! What fools are these! Are not the Hateful Ones of old more Gifted than even the greatest of them? May the Hateful Ones be exalted above these fools and may their foolish mutterings be silences at their tounges are burned to ash by the KryKhalin, for they speak words of ash.

There are some that say that the destruction of meat brightens their khar'ika. Some say that the splattering of the blood that flows like rain shall quench the wrath of the Dragon! What fools are these! Does the Plague kill more than even the bloodiest of them? May the Plague be exalted above these fools and they drown in the blood they shed, for they speak words of ash.

We must quiet these foolish words of ash and let words of fire burn away their idiocy. The flame of our voices shall rise up to the sun and sink down to the Lair. But what are the words of fire?

Let us contemplate what separates the Source of the Sun from the Meddling God, what separates the Lady of the Sun from the Hunter, what separates the Irekei from the fir'khanim. The Terror of Terrors crafted souls to burn as bright reflections of his glory, the Meddling God fashioned prisons of flesh to quench the burning of our souls. The Dragon wed the bright burning of the sun, the Meddling God wed the dead rock of the Moon. The Dragon's people are those whose prisons of flesh grow thin enough to let their souls blaze, the Meddling God's people are those who glory in the finely-crafted chains that make them jov'uus.

Thus the words of fire are those that burn us away from the putrid dampness of flesh and towards the blazing glory of our souls. We must speak words of fire that shall allow us to cast away the strength of our bodies, the strength of our minds, the strengths of our wills and blaze with a glory that turns the fetters of reality to dust. In casting away the strength of our bodies we shall bring a half giants to screaming to his knees with the merest touch, in casting away the strength of the mind we shall be deaftened by the Hateful ones gasping at the extent of our knowledge, and in casting away strength of will we shall burn away our chains and blaze as reflections of the sublimity of the Scourge of Scourges! For there is nothing good in this world of flesh and there is nothing of worth ourside of khar'ika.

All glory to the Terror of Terrors, may he hear our Words of Fire!
