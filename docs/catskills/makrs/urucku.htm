Mak'r Rules -- Urucku and Ranks -- Mak'r Paths

Urucku: Dedication and Purpose
Buorca, which stands for Selfless Teamwork, represents the attitude of the Mak'r Bloodline. In order to be a Mak'r, one must be willing to dedicate endless time to hard work. However, one may expect help and teamwork from the entire Bloodline. Each Mak'r should support one another, and indeed the rest of Shadowclan participates in the teamwork as well. The entire Clan gathers resources as Mak'rs work to provide what Shadowclan needs. As Orcs are not allowed to use items made by non-orc hands, Mak'rs are kept busy on a daily basis. It is tough work, but rewarding, for orcs will always remember a good weapon that led to a victory in battle.

The Buorca Shrine is located in the center of the Orc Mining Caves in Yew. It is a short run along the mountains northeast of the Yew Orc Fort. All Mak'rs must be familiar with this place. It is where offerings are given to Wargod in selfless sacrifice. It is the place where Buorca is taught.

Ranks: Overviews, Rules, Equipment
All Mak'rs wear the brown half apron regardless of rank. All Mak'rs may choose to wear a regular orc helmet. All Mak'rs, excluding Nubkuu, may wear an evil orc helmet. No Mak'r, with the exception of Big Mak'r, is allowed at any time any type of human attire, which includes kilts, pants, and shirts.

Nubkuu Mak'rs:
Overview: Nubkuu is the beginning rank. It is expected that a Mak'r choose his/her Path at this rank, get added to the guildstone, and begin to help at the fort.

Rules: May not attack anyone, even Gruntees. If you are attacked by a Gruntee you may defend yourself or run. If attacked by a grunt or higher you may not defend nor run.

Equipment: Orc mask, leather gloves, half apron (brown), leather pants, gruntee boots. Feurks can wear also wear leather skirt and a regular leather top.
Tala Mak'rs:
Overview: Tala has visited the Buorca shrine and knows about the urucku. He/she is progressing in helping orcs around the fort. Tala, although Tribeless, may not be Tribed without the Big Mak'r's permission.

Rules: May attack a Gruntee with reason. May not attack anyone else. If attacked by Tribeless Grunt, you may defend or run. If attacked by a Tribed you may not defend but you can run. If attacked by Bloodleader or Nob you may not defend or run.

Equipment: Orc mask, regular leather gorget, leather tunic, full apron (dark gray), leather gloves, half apron (brown), leather pants, long boots (brown). Feurks may also wear leather skirt and regular leather top.
Shakh Mak'rs:
Overview: Shakh has interest in seeking out Tribing. At this rank, the Mak'r does not need permission to be Tribed.

Rules: May attack gruntees and Tribeless if it is for a good reason. If attacked by a fellow tribed Grunt, you may defend or run. If attacked by a Blood Leader or Nob you may not defend or run.

Equipment: Orc mask, leather gorget, leather arms, leather tunic, full apron (light gray until Tribed), leather gloves, half apron (brown), leather pants, long boots (short if Buorca, Tribeless brown until accepted in Tribe). Feurks may also wear leather skirt and regular leather top.
Tribed Mak'rs:
Overview: Chosen and Tribed, the Mak'r is considered to be a Tribed Grunt to other Bloodlines.

Equipment: Orc mask, leather gorget, leather arms, leather tunic, full apron (color of Tribe), leather gloves, half apron (brown), leather pants, long boots (short if Buorca, color of Tribe). Feurks may also wear leather skirt and leather top.
Buorca Mak'rs:
Overview: Buorca has the skill to teach and pass on the message of the urucku Buorca. He/she has taken lower ranked Mak'rs to the Buorca Shrine and explained the life of a Mak'r.

Equipment: Orc mask, leather gorget, leather arms, leather tunic, full apron (color of tribe), leather gloves, half apron (brown), leather pants, short boots (color of tribe if Tribed). Feurks may also wear leather skirt and leather top.
Durub Mak'rs:
Overview: Proven service and progress in Buorca leads to Durub. He/she has taught many lower ranked Mak'rs about Buorca and has shown over time his/her own selflessness to the Bloodline and Shadowclan. Durub may take on certain designated tasks, such as responsibility over a section of resources.

Equipment: Orc mask, studded gorget, leather arms, leather tunic, full apron (color of tribe), leather gloves, half apron (brown), leather pants, short boots (color of tribe if Tribed). Feurks may include the leather skirt, leather top and/or studded shorts with this.
Mog'ob'goth Mak'r:
Overview: Time and time again, a Mog'ob'goth has proven his/her worth to Mak'rs and Shadowclan. He/she is heavily involved in the Bloodline's well-being and organization. He/she may take on secondary responsibilities for the Big Mak'r. In the absence of the Big Mak'r's presence, the Mog'ob'Goth may give quests, promote, and designate responsibilities in the Bloodline.

Equipment: Orc mask, studded gorget, studded arms, full apron (tribe color), studded gloves, studded tunic, half apron, studded pants, short boots (tribe color). Feurks may add on any of the feurk studded leather.
Big Mak'r:
Overview: Ultimately responsible for the improvement of the Bloodline, the Big Mak'r must be organized and influential. Tasks include BOD organization, resource inventory, vendor tracking, and promotion within the Bloodline. Often, the Big Mak'r handles Drinkie Fests and Clan Treasury.

Equipment: Orc mask, studded gorget, bone arms, studded tunic, full apron (color of tribe), studded gloves, half apron (brown), studded pants, short boots (color of tribe). Feurks may add on any of the feurk studded leather.
  Sharkuu Mak'r:
Equipment: Same as the Big Mak'rs, but may use ringmail.
