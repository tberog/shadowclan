Rules for Visitors Seeking a Peaceful Visit to the Orc Fort
Written by Brother Ren (Updated)



Do what ever an orc tells you to do. Doesn't matter if its a gruntee fresh from the womb. If not, you had better leave fast.

NEVER loot a corpse that has fallen at the fort. If a fight happens, hide, run, or recall out.  Orc fights are not your fights, no matter how good a friend you are.

Never enter the fort without a Grunt or higher ranked Orc's permission.  Usually tribute will get you in, depending on your intentions.

If you show up at the fort and ask for entrance, it is a good idea to appear non-hostile.  Removing of your weapon and helm are appropriate measures.  Also leave your steed outside of the fort.  Orcs love horse meat.

Never at any time play clompgame with an orc of any rank.  Nobs won't ask you, grunts know better, and the pee-brained gruntee's will learn quickly.  If you play a clompgame with one orc, you play it with all of them.

Do not participate in combat at the fort unless an orc nob requests aid.

Roleplay! The orcs are doing it, you better do it. Bring a tribute to their Wargod. This should grant you safe passage through orc lands.

Bad idea to summon a daemon or polymorph into one. This is the form of their wargod and they will interpret your action as mocking them.

Don't flame on the clan message board.  Post a respectable message, and they will respond.  If you have a problem with another orc, tell a Nob.  Nobs can be reconized by their body sash. The best way to complain is to send a nob an icq message.

Don't go spamming the fort. At best, you will be put on ignore. That's not the way to handle it. Use some common sense.  Keep in mind, they are orcs.
