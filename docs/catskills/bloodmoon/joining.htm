Becoming a Convert
First decide if Blood Moon fits your character's overall current personality. By the ideals of Blood Moon orcs, you do not choose what Tribe you belong to, it is your destiny. By having this attitude and treating your orc character as though he/she is meant to be Blood Moon, you will find immediate responses from higher ranks seeking out Blood Moon potentials. Other ideas on how to establish yourself as a role playing oriented orc are listed below.

Blood Tome: This is your orc's own personal writing about his/her experience following Shakh Buurz, along with discoveries or quests. Some refer to it as an equivalent of an orc bible. It is something that Blood Moon orcs generally keep within their Tribe, since it is very sacred to them. These are generally written in "newborn" books, so that they always remain with you. If you have left yours behind, ask a Blood Moon member if they can help you get one.

Sacrifices: Offer sacrifices to Shakh Buurz any chance you get. Bring animals to the fort and have massive slaughters and prayers. Whenever you kill something, make the body bleed onto the ground. Remember that Blood Moon orcs believe that the more blood is spilled into the earth, the stronger Shakh Buurz will become, until the end day arrives and the Wargod rises. Eat the hearts of heathens, for it will renew your strength in battle.

Castes: If you plan to be quite ahead of yourself, you should look into what Caste you would like your character to be in the future. These are meant to be suited to your character's behaviors. For example, an orc who prefers to dwell in the dreary forests of Yew bringing about visions through days of hunger would be better suited to the Prophet role than to the Martyr role. Figure out stories that will reflect your character's active part in a particular Caste.

 

Life as a Convert
 Although your character is still Tribeless, you have the benefit of being acknowledged as a Blood Moon hopeful through your title. It means you have achieved the first step in becoming Tribed and devoting yourself to a Caste. Continue to stress role playing above all. Feel free to preach if your character is a Preacher, and charge ahead clomping if your character is a Martyr. Work on developing your character through activity around the fort and the posting of stories.
