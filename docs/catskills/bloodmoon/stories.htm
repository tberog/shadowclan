Youthful Aspirations - Puguzg'zalu

Grey suddenly covered the ground in a wave of light... All color and sound eventually being consumed.
Puguzg'zalu looked down as the grey figures raced away from the corpse. Sudden understanding filled him as he identified it as his own.
"Okay.. So I just find a healer" Pug had fallen numerous times before, but he could never get use to the feeling... or perhaps more correctly said, lack of feeling. He searched mindlessly looking for one of the Wargods disciples to ressurect him. A figure could be seen in the trees, so he raced toward it.

The sound and color returned to him, as he materialized into a solid form. Pug sat up, and gave the orb linked to his necklace a quick lick, which gave him a feeling of magical refreshment, taking away the pain.

"Arrgg....Damn Head Biters.. That's it... I've had it with those tools of ignorance and laziness."
It only took a blink of an eye to return back to the fort, but this time.. he was prepared.
His constant mixing of various ingrediants had given him the knowledge of many potions and elixirs.. but he really only used one. This potion was not for him though.. it was for his enemies.. . The potions swirling with purple and yellow particles in it were tightly stuffed into his robe, leaving barely enough room for himself to even be in it.
As Pug walked into the fort he noticed a group of Head Biters standing on the ramp, and he calmly walked infront of their discussions to address them.
"What do you want? Foolish Mage!" The group of orcs laughed together as if something truely hilarious was said.
"I have come to prey for you, so good times may come to the orcs" He wasn't lieing... He thought he was doing the Shadowclan a favor in the destruction of this tribe of mislead orcs.

"Why do we need -you- to prey for us?" A lanky, spear holding Head Biter asked.

"Because without the Lord, you are lost...."
A look of anger filled all of the orcs gathered around the ramp to the elevated hut. Pug could see this.. and quickly let loose a religous chant of sacrifice.
"Hoowah Lord of Darkness,
Take these deaths as our apologies to you,
The clan is the water, and you are the river,
Hooowah.... Hoooowah!!!"
The orcs had nearly reached him with their birage of spears and axes, but they were to late.
Pug clutched his orb and spouted the words of power "Port Ort Grav".

The shock of lightening filled his body and proceeded to the endless power of exploding potions surounding himself....and then there was darkness.

"Follow the path of blood my child...
only then will you get to your true ability.
Devotion is the key, not stupidity.."
The coarse voice whispered in Pug's dreams..
"Wake up my disciple, you will have your time... but not yet..."
Pug opened his eyes suddenly and looked up at the aged Nob staring down at him.
"Sorry about that mage, they didn't listen to me."
"I will remmember it..." Pug whispered quietly and gathered his health.
"Is this a vision from the Lord Of Darkness? Has he pledged me great power in the future?"
Pug's thoughts raced through the rest of the night, but he know he would never know.. until his time came.

 

Sacrificial Ignorance - Puguzg'zalu

Ignorance
"Drink mighty Pug! Show us your great power!" The humans chanted over and over. Pug quickly downed the jugs of cider as he listened to the numerous praises being shouted at him by the humans.

"Lineup humies!" came a booming voice from behind the wobbling Pug. Zatush'faugh took a step confidently in front of the humans as they scattered chaotically trying to line up. "Orcs hungry! Git Orcs fuud agh wool at bah bah pens!" he commanded.

The humans stood dumbfounded. "What did it say?"
Pug thrusted his staff up in the air screaming to the humans, noticing their confusion, "Diz wai! Follow da mite ub pug tu git fuud!" He marched in front of the humans and led them ignorantly.

"There is food!" said one of the humans behind him. He eagerly looked and saw at the corner of his eye that he was now partially surrounded by the humans. Pug hurled his orb of power over their heads and magically appeared under it, catching it as it fell. As he caught the ball a firm blow to the back from a war hammer sent him forward. Only being met by the sharp blade of a kryss pushing through his abdomen.
"Arrrgh!" Pug cried out in pain as he slowly slid from the blade, falling to the ground.

"Take the ball and let's get out of here!"
A human quickly snatched the magical orb from Pug's relaxed grasp and followed the other humans into a gate.

Pain
Staring into the sky motionless, the world seeming to move slowly.
"Pug!?" screamed the gruntee Hav'grim on her way to his rescue.

Hav'grim strained as she picked him up to take him to the orc fort.
As she entered there were only a few orcs in the fort. The rest of the clan obviously in pursuit of the attackers. Hav'grim quickly searched the fort for a proper place to let Pug recuperate. She sat him down on a bedroll next to the fort's anvil.

For some reason there was a deep longing for him to grip his magical orb, but to his shock he found that it was not in his arms. A cry of anger attempted to escape his lips, but was only audible as a deep wheezing sound. He knew there would be much trouble for him if he did not find it soon.

Pug pushed up on his staff, attempted to struggle to his feet, only to be forced back down by the young Zult-R. Pug gripped the staff even tighter as he sneered at Hav'grim, forcefully pushing to his feet. He felt alone without his magical orb, and he set out to find it.

The vast woods surrounding the fort were usually easily navigable for Pug. Everything seemed so different to him though. The trees were taller, and seemed to weave back and forth to taunt him. Pug wheezed in anger as he shook a fist at the lively forest. He searched through the forest, trying to find his way to the one place he knew would be safe from the trees. The house had stood up to the great power of the forest for ages. He turned left as he saw a grave sticking out of the ground. Exhausted and angry Pug kneeled before the stone. Scraping his fingers lightly over the carving in the stone.
"ZugRak, crayzee mojoka" read the stone.

" I am here" Pug thought as he forced himself back to his feet.
He passed several trees and a worn hut was unhidden by the forest. Pug continued to the worn home of past mojokas.

Sacrifice
"Where is lat..where is lat?" Pug rocked back and forth in the stone chair within the battered walls. He reached to the air as if he could suddenly bring back the only object that had previously helped him control his powers. A loud noise erupted from behind Pug as he rocked back and forth. Three other orcs had entered the room, slamming the door behind them.

"Me hab sumfin for lat. It am sumfin dat Gu'clomp teached me long ago" exclaimed Gi'mar, reaching around in his robe searching for something. "Mmhm..yub...mmhm..."
The other two mojokas, which had entered the room with him, stared at Gi'mar as he spoke with his magical bunny. The bunny made a final nod to Gi'mar and quickly jumped straight onto Pug, crawling into his robe.

"Lat beddur gib him back, or me will be gone for good!" Suddenly Pug felt the bunny twitching and pushing against his body, and Gi'mar erupted into flames, falling to the ground.
"Whoa!" The mojokas gasped, following the lump crawling in Pug's robes with their eyes.

 

Of Arrows - Lud'rak

Visionary. This is how Lud'rak might describe it, in a single word too big and round for her thin mouth to contain. Smoke spirals wafting from an orcish firepit, the sky dim in its sunset of pale orange and touches of yellow. Dark red-orange highlights of trees without leaves swaying against the dominance of the wind, looking as if the limbs might snap from the rebellion of force.

Below her, at the edge of the descending ramp, there stood five orcish gruntees--Yggdrazil the Ligz'r, Zagrukh the Kigg'r, Zeht'baal the Kigg'r, Wuz'Rug the Zult'r, and Zuljin the Kigg'r. Some were aged wiser than the others, Zagrukh having only recently come into the world, still slick from his rise from the uzg and the belly of his birthing.

"Whu ere tinkz dem deztined tu be ogh da uldezt Tribe, da strungezt in da weyz ogh Shakh Buurz, da Bluud Muuniez?" Lud'rak lifted her chin and proposed the question to the small gathering, as a heathen of orc skin shifted noisily in the wooden pen, and the firelight dimmed and gradually dissipated to red embers and ash. For too long, there was silence, uneasy in the air that smelled of rotting onions and burnt human hair.

There was one who raised his paw, a zult'r. Lud'rak grinned and arched an eyebrow while assessing the rest of the gathering. Many of them were unsure of which Tribes they belonged to, but this zult'r seemed rather convinced that he knew his path, even as young as he was. Lud'rak knew the same feeling, something warm and dark in the very pit of her stomach, expelling itself from her throat with every word she murmured in the name of Shakh Buurz. This was her way, her fate, and she was pleased to see it in another orc.

"Wull den, sinze sum ogh lat du nub gruk whut lat want tu be ash muun," although granted Lud'rak was looking as one gruntee fanatic about the fire god of the Ghaashies, she continued on with her words, "Me gib lat ash chanze tu blah wit Wargod." And in that moment, her eyes closed, and she whispered a prayer so heated on her tongue that it made her teeth click and her jaw clenched tight.

The glide of her arms eased the bow forward, and she prepared for the strike. Across her hips rested a satchel bound with leather strips at the sides, and in this, she carried her arrows. Five arrows were removed, one for each gruntee, and she held them by her strong, knicked fingers. The five arrows were shot at once. Each one slammed into the gruntees, hard and fowl with the intent to do great harm.

Lud'rak's eyes opened, and she saw which orc was chosen from the few. While one single arrow was lodged into the chest of Wuz'Rug, piercing beyond his ringmail armor and into his flesh, the other four were left with arrows in their thighs or feet. Wuz'Rug gasped, but remained strong, as Lud'rak took the time to step down the ramp and harshly pull the bloody-tipped arrow from his wounded skin.

"Whut am lat, den, Wuz'Rug the zult'r?" Lud'rak's eyes narrowed for a second, as she was curious in what ways Wargod would speak to her in acknowledging the one she knew she much teach the methods of Blood Moon to. He was of a bloodline from other lands, and because of this, Lud'rak was admittedly suspicious of any trickery that might come into play by his kind. Clever as she was, she sought to never be deceived.

"Prophit!" Wuz'Rug cried it out with such confidence that Lud'rak was stunned. She stepped back and eyed up the others, who were greatly anxious for bootcamp. Oh, and what an interesting night it would be, for a first time of running bootcamp, and a first chance at teaching what being a Prophet of Shakh Buurz wholly meant to the entirety of the Clan to one zult'r.

But the blood red moon glowed so brightly it could be seen through the veil of smoke that hung in the air like a death shroud.
