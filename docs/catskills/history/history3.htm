Part Three: Blakblud Rebellion

 

Blakbluds

The Blakblud Rebellionsaccording to ScumGlug, BlakBlud Nob and Thrak-Ghaash founder and Buurzgoth, BlakBlud Wa'Ligzr and Thrak-Ghaash Blud Ligz-R.

The First Blakblud Rebellion:

Various members from several different tribes decided that they didn't like the way things were going in the Shadowclan (specifically, friendly relations with some humies and humie guilds, and the Undead) and decided to leave their own tribes and form a new tribe with the goal of bringing the orcs back to their roots.

Zug'Zuul, the Hedbiter nob, was joined by gug'gug, ScumGlug, Ka'Cal, and quite a few other clanmembers (mainly from the Hedbiter Tribe) to form the Blakbluds.

The Blakblud tribe grew and prospered, attracting quite a few new members, and doing well in the Warboss clomps. Zug'Zuul went to join the WarGod and ScumGlug, Ka'Cal, and Kil'rogg were eventually promoted as nobs of the Blakblud tribe.

Kil'rogg's account of the first Rebellion is here: Blakblud [Rebel Tribe]

The Second Blakblud Rebellion:

Five or so Drinkie Fests after the creation and rise of the Blakbluds, there came the infamous Warboss Clomp where Glug Ughurk, the Howler candidate was healed by a Dark Dwarf named Grim Stormcloud during the combat. There was a huge outcry, and the Blakbluds demanded that the Warboss Clomp be re-fought.  The rest of the tribes were willing to forget the stuntie interference, as it mattered little in the overall outcome and let Glug keep the title.

The Blakbluds were not willing to allow the Warboss to keep his new office without a rematch. When the Clan returned to the fort, the Blakbluds refused to follow his orders, and when the rest of the clan tried to force them, they fought.  Over the next couple of days, as it became obvious that the rest of the clan was willing to keep Glug as Warboss, the Blakbluds declared their own candidate, N'Grulk, to be the Warboss, and the Blakblud Rebellion was in full swing.

For several days, the Blakbluds took on the entire Clan. Tribal conflicts are not uncommon, and this was especially true for BlakBluds as they often fought with the Howlers and Moonies, but this one became something different..  Having had enough of the fighting (and losing, because the Blakbluds were severely outnumbered), gug'gug unilaterally decided (without consulting the other Blakblud nobs at all) to split the Blakbluds from the clan. Because of the strict hierarchy in the Blakbluds and the oaths of loyalty, the other Blakbluds were forced to follow.

gug'gug moved his tribe to the caves in the Shame Pass and declared many new and strange rules for his Blakblud clan. Many of the other Blakbluds disagreed with this development, including the two other Nobs in the tribe, ScumGlug and Ka'Cal.  Many exiled Blakbluds chose one of two paths - they left the Blakbluds and rejoined the Shadowclan as tribeless, or they remained Blakbluds but went to sleep, hoping that things would get better and they could re-awaken and return.

For a week or so, gug'gug tried to lead his clan-in-exile, and then he disappeared. After another week or so of gug'gug being gone, ScumGlug stepped forward, claimed that gug'gug had died, and that ScumGlug had received a revelation from the Wargod - he was to form a new tribe, the Thrak-Ghaash, from the remains of the Blakbluds.

Most of the exiled Blakbluds (that had not already returned as tribeless) followed ScumGlug back into the Clan as this new tribe.  The next Warboss clomp (won by the Moonie, Grishnak)  was a day or two after that. The Thrak-Ghaash participated, but didn't have enough eligible warriors to fill out a full team.
