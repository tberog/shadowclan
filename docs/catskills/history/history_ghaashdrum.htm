After some discussion and trading of gold and jewels we traded for the drum. The orks returned home Once there I was placed in charge of the drum for a week then gave it to Kargrath to put on his vendor. It was then loaned to Scumglug and Smugruk for a Thrak-ghaash ceremony eventually put on the Thrak-Ghaash vendor who died in a mysterious accident leaving the drum on the ground for who knows how long. Smug picked it up and then the Moonies challenged the Thrak-Ghaash for its possession. In a battle in the undead keep the Bloodmoon orks killed all the thrak-ghaash and now we possess the drum. Grishnak currently is its guardian.  The gaash drum was lost when the Orcpost collapsed.

This was the spot.
He was sure of it. The years had passed, and like any other mortal, he had felt his mental faculties deteriorating over the time.
Maybe he could not remember what he had eaten for breakfast, but he hadn't forgotten that night.
He hated it here. The bitter cold that blew in over the surrounding water was more than anyone of his advanced age should have to endure. How he ever actually lived in this cold at one time was beyond his ken.
Clearing his mind of any thoughts, he closed his eyes and tried to let the memory lead him. Tentatively, he took one step. Another.
The feeling was strong now. Soon enough, he stopped and pulled out an aged bag.
He knelt down and ran his hand over the snow covering the ground.
Yes indeed, this was the spot. 
He untied the drawstring that sealed the bag and pulled out a weathered, beaten orc helm.
He placed the orc helm upon the ground and ran his hand over it. This was a helm that had obviously seen many battles.
He sat beside the orc helm and waited.
Nothing happened. He sighed and tried to remember....
At some point he must have fallen asleep, shivering in the cold.
He was awakened by the mournful howl of one of the many white wolves that made this island their home. He must have been asleep for awhile, the sun had set and all was black.
He stood up and turned around. 
There was a wolf there, staring at him.
Uncomprehending, he tilted his head and watched the wolf. The wolf circled around to the spot where he had placed the helm.

"Da uruks will take da drum. Gib it tu Glug.

"I know you will use the power of the drum for your purposes. I am glad that those purposes suit my own, Glug. I know it is too much to ask that any orc would trust any human, as it should be. Feed the drum well Glug. Feed it well with the blood of the human scourge, Glug."

He tried to speak, but something was making it impossible, the best he could manage was a strangled gurgling.
He lifted his hand to his face to wipe the sweat from his eyes and realized somehow the orc helm had ended up on his head.

The heat had become intolerable, more than any human could bear. Pain was taking away his consciousness. He tried to remove the helm, but somehow it was evading his grasp.
