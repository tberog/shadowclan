Underground Kobold Origin Story

Since the inception of the three realms there has existed two worlds. Neither the surface dwellers nor those in the deep underground knew that the other existed, and this was the state of things untill recently. Reality as defined by the underworld inhabitants had always begun at the core of the earth, and ended several hundred miles short of the deepest surface caves that were, as of this point, unexplored. Birag'tuek, the name of the place called home by the dark kobolds also housed several other sentient races including the dark dwarves and the trolls.

Because of the harsh conditions beneath the earth's crust, resources were hard to come by, and not in enough quantity to support the now burgeoning races. Peace was easy to maintain when the three races where young and of small number, but with all three groups growing in size and complexity, tensions began to mount. Eventually war broke out between the trolls and kobolds as they occupied similar areas and were in the most direct competition with one another.

At this point, roughly 500 years from the present, a kobold warrior culture emerged. The kobolds, being weaker but far more crafty than the trolls managed to outmaneuver the superior troll armies and eventualy succeded in pushing them up into the outer reaches of Birag'tuek. Those trolls who stayed or were captured became slaves. After the first great war a period of peace ensued for about 100 to 120 years where the kobolds were free to expand and grow in the spaces previously occupied by the trolls. Resistance forces and freedom forces where a constant threat, and conflict was a daily routine for the Kobolds. Throughout this time the ancient kobolds developed the majority of their warrior culture, religion and political structure.

These ancient Kobolds determined themselves to be the chosen race of Herga (In kobold means the Warrior father), and determined that their strengths and powers come from themselves and the earth to submit all those in existance to the Kerchat (divine order). The divine order called for the Kobolds to control all of Herga's creation as only they have the capacity to care for it properly, and that all other races must either submit as slaves or be killed. Also, Kerchat treats all kobolds as a single being that eminates from Herga. The kobolds believe that they make up the earthly body of their god, and that this body, in order to function, must exist as a unified force in the world. So in some sense, there is a trinity of caretaker, conqurer and divinity in the principles of Kerchat followed by underworld kobolds. This suggests that submission to the horde should yield one not of that race to be spared, and this is indeed the case. Though kobolds see other races as far inferior to their own, they will not kill for sport.

The kobolds of the underworld were governed by a single council of generals. According to the principles of Kerchat, both magic and the ability to fight stem from the same source and are therefore different expressions of the same thing; the force of Herga. This council was therefore populated by the oldest and most respected warriors and magicians.

Subsequent to the revelation of Kihagh (the faith (aka bloodlust)), a holy war ensued with the dark dwarves who had, for some time lived without the need to fight major battles. The result was complete subjugation of the dwarf race with a portion of the dwarves fleeing to the banished zone in the upper reaches of Birag'tuek. At this point in time the Kobold civilization was massive and thriving, with huge cities twice the size of any that existed on the surface, expanding ever deeper into the core of Birag'tuek.

The outer underworld, on the other hand, was populated by primitive groups of dark trolls, small armed settlements of dark dwarves, and the occasional roving band of outcast Kobolds -- for not all Kobolds heard the voice of Herga and followed the unifying principles of Kerchat. From time to time, the great Kobold civilization sent forces into the upper zones to capture and punish the degenerate Kobolds, and to keep the other races from growing stronger.

Humans and surface dwarves began to mine the earth for its metals and minerals. Some of these mines reached deep, near the upper parts of Birag'tuek, and thus began the era of human encounters with the inhabitants of the underworld. Each of the underworld races reacted differently. The dark Trolls reacted with simple, uncoordinated violence. The dark dwarves reacted by withdrawing even more completely into their small, fortified settlements. The outcast Kobolds, fitting their native craftiness, began to shadow the Humans, steal from them, and cause trouble.

Some bands of these outcast Kobolds were so weak and unable to fend for themselves in the dark underworld that they became dependent on following and stealing from Humans, and developed an uneasy pariah relationship. Like domesticated dogs, the once independent outcast Kobolds became pathetic followers of the Humans, sneaking around their settlements, sometimes stealing, and sometimes even doing chores for the humans in exchange for scraps. Others became enslaved by Humans and surface dwarves in the mines. These degenerate, weak Kobolds are the ancestors of today's surface Kobolds.

Meanwhile, the great Kobold civilization had burrowed even deeper into the earth, and had become more self-absorbed. Its military forces, sent rarely now into the upper zones, had to take longer and longer journeys to accomplish their missions and return to the deep homeland -- generations in many cases, because of the sheer vastness of the underworld. These forces became more self sufficient, forming a social structure of nomadic tribes, with both male and female warriors and magicians. Yet unlike the degenerate surface Kobolds, these clans stayed true to the principles of Kerchat. We, the Shadowclan, began as one of these military clans.

No one knows why the great underground eruptions occurred, if they were natural, or stemmed from some magical source or accident. All we know is that their effects were huge, cutting off the deepest parts of Birag'tuek from the upper zones, and causing terrible destruction in the middle zones, where we were stationed. Surely the great civilization of the underground Kobolds is not gone, for Herga would not allow such a terrible thing; but we have no more contact with it. Surely there are other military Clans that were cut off as we were, or lost Kobolds from such clans, that we may join with in order to carry out our joint mission. We must find these lost Kobolds, subjugate and conquer the surface world, and then find a way to rejoin our great civilization.
