Non-Member FAQ

I'm a Kobold. Can I make friends with Shadowclan?

Neutral Kobolds may be tolerated by the Shadowclan, sometimes group with Shadowclan members, and may seek our protection.

You are a "neutral Kobold" if you

  • are not a member of an enemy guild (all members of enemy guilds are killed on sight)
  • are alone, or traveling only with other neutral Kobolds
  • do not act in an aggressive or insulting manner

Even neutral Kobolds should use caution around the Shadowclan. If you are seen as a threat or your guild is attacking us elsewhere, you will be attacked. If others arrive and a fight breaks out, you may be seen as threatening and attacked, you may be accidentally targeted, or you may fall within the range of our AOE spells. If you truly want to be safe around the Shadowclan, join the Clan!

I'm not a Kobold, but I like Shadowclan. Can I make friends with Shadowclan?

No. Shadowclan Kobolds are xenophobic and do not make friends with members of other races. Other races will be asked to pay tribute and then expected to leave the area. If you like us and want to be around the Clan, make a Kobold character. Better yet, make a Kobold character and join the Clan!

What does "tribute" mean?

If you're not a Kobold, and you're not in an enemy guild, you will be asked to pay tribute. In a typical encounter, you'll be swarmed by a bunch of kobolds, and probably either mezzed or rooted. They'll be screaming "PAY TRIBUT TU SHADUWKLAN!" and other variations.

How do I pay the tribute?

Do not act threatening, or you'll be attacked. Don't move, even if mez wears off! Do not sit down or run away. Look for the kobold standing right in front of you, pay him something, and wait for a moment to make sure the kobolds are satisfied. Until the other kobolds know you paid, if you try to leave, they'll think you're trying to escape, and they'll attack. Don't move until you hear the kobolds start yelling "HOOOOWAH TRIBUT!" and various other things.

How much tribute do I have to pay?

It doesn't have to be much, far less than buying back con. You can offer money or an item. If you are poor, just give whatever you can. Of course, if you're high level, and pay us a few copper, that will be insulting and we'll kill you. Standard fee is approximately the cost of one point of constitution, but we enjoy roleplaying. A shiny rock given as tribute can make an enjoyable encounter for everyone.

Can I hang around after I pay tribute?

No. Once you've paid, get out of the area. Your tribute allows you to pass through the area alive, that's all. Non-Kobolds are not allowed to hang around Shadowclan, no matter how much tribute they pay.

Why was I asked to pay tribute again?

Paying tribute does not make you a friend. If you encounter more than one group of Shadowclan, you may need to pay tribute to both groups.

Why didn't I get a chance to pay tribute?

If you appear to be a threat, you will be attacked rather than asked to pay tribute.

You will be seen as a threat if you

  • are a member of an enemy guild
  • travel with members of enemy guilds
  • have recently attacked Clan members
  • behave in an aggressive or insulting manner
  • begin to cast a spell or run
  • do not leave the area after paying tribute

You may also be seen as a threat for other reasons, such as being high level, traveling in a large group, or traveling with enchanter pets.

When encountering the Shadowclan, you can increase your chances of survival if you immediately yell "I PAY TRIBUTE" and act non-threatening (do not move, ready your weapon, or cast spells).

Why is my guild on Shadowclan's enemy list?

Guilds whose members consistently attack Shadowclan members are put on our enemy list.

How can my guild be removed from Shadowclan's enemy list?

Guilds who stop attacking our members and approach Shadowclan in game may be removed from our enemy list. Note that we do not resolve such issues out of game; you must approach us in game, and negotiations will take place from there.

What should I do if a Shadowclan member does not seem to be acting in accordance with Shadowclan rules?

Shadowclan enforces its rules among its members. If you believe a Shadowclan member is breaking Shadowclan rules, please report the incident to one of our officers (called "Nobs").
