Rules of Appropriate Blah

 

Appropriate Use of Blah Boards (message boards)

Your message board name should be the same as your character's name. If you have several characters, use the name of your main character. If you reroll or switch main characters, change your message board posting name.or register under the new name.

Do not cross post. Post only once, in the most appropriate forum.

No personal attacks or flaming. Be respectful of fellow players both in and out of Shadowclan.


Appropriate Use of Clan Blah (guild chat, /gu)

Shadowclan guild chat is used differently from other guilds, so be sure to read the following guidelines carefullty. Also remember that Shadowclan guild chat must be kept in character at all times.

Members abusing guild chat may be temporarily muted (lose guild chat privileges).


DO NOT do the following things on guild chat:

Do not use guild chat to find groups. To look for a learning group, use /gc who to find people relatively near you age and speak with them directly or through your Klomp Rank chat group. If a large trip is in progress, use the chat group for the trip. If you are already near the Horde and need to be added to a group, use /say or /yell.

Do not use guild chat to run your group or trip. Use /yell, group chat, or the chat group for the trip.

Do not ask on guild chat if someone can add a new member. Most Nobs are busy enough that they'll miss the message. Use /gc who, find which Nobs are awake, and /send them your request privately.

Do not use ALL CAPS on guild chat. All-caps on guild chat is reserved for Nobs and leaders of large raids, and even then only for important announcements or emergencies. Getting ganked is not an emergency.

Do not discuss anything out of character (OOC) over guild chat, such as realm points, item stats, specialization points, experience points, amount of damage done, and the like. Even if you are using kobo blah, discussion that is OOC in content is forbidden in all channels except /send. Take all OOC discussions to /send or the message boards.


Here is what you SHOULD use guild chat for:

Greeting the Clan. Say "Ug" when you log on, and "Gug'yu" when you log off.

Greeting new Kobolds. Say "Hoowah" and "Ug" to new kobos who just joined.

Cheering. Cheer for people being promoted or for other happy news.

Friendly banter with others in the Clan who are not in your area or group. However, don't get into an argument on guild chat.

Occasional questions. Try asking in /send first, but if the people you're asking in /send can't seem to answer, you may ask on /gu. Remember however that you can't ask OOC questions on /gu, such as questions about points or stats.

Significant in-character achievements such as attaining a level or being able to craft a new type of material. Note that realm points are not in-character.

Reports of major enemy groups entering an area that has a lot of clannies. Be specific. What guild, and where are they. Be brief, don't tell the story about what just happened, and don't yell it in all caps.

General announcements. If anyone is welcome to join a group or raid that is forming to go hunt, broadcast the announcement on /gu. Be specific. Tell where you are going, and who they should contact if they want to come along.

Chat group announcements. Leaders of large trip chat groups may periodically announce that people should contact them to be added to the chat group.

Crafter announcements, such as announcing that you are in Jordheim ready to make equipment. Crafters should also make sure to use /setwho trade while they are actively crafting, so that everyone can see them in the /gc who listing.
